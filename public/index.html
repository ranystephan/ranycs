Rany Stephan

I am a graduate student at Stanford University at the Institute for Computational and Mathematical Engineering. My work focuses on applying quantitative and computational techniques to solve complex problems, from computational biology to mathematical finance.

About Me | CV | Research | Projects | Notes | Publications

Research Experience

  • Convex Optimization for Portfolio Transition (cvxtransition) (June 2025 – Present)
    • Advisor: Prof. Stephen Boyd
    • Developing portfolio transition strategies using Model Predictive Control (MPC) and convex optimization to minimize implementation shortfall while tracking a benchmark. Manuscript in progress.
  • Fixed Income Modeling (Dec 2024 – Present)
    • Advisor: Prof. Darrell Duffie
    • Modeling off-the-run bond inventories and dealer behavior by formulating and solving Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman (HJB) equations to derive analytically-grounded optimal control. Manuscript in progress.
  • AI and ML in Computational Pathology (HExIF) (Jan 2025 – Present)
    • Advisor: Dr. Andrew Gentles
    • Leading the development of HExIF, an end-to-end PyTorch pipeline that generates virtual multiplex immunofluorescence images from H&E stains, reducing analysis time and cost. Manuscript in preparation.
  • Machine Learning, NLP & Time-Series Analysis (Sept 2022 – Aug 2024)
    • Co-authored ‘Opportunities for circular economy in waste reuse: Insights from social media data mining’, a journal paper in Resources, Conservation & Recycling. Applied transfer learning on over 10,000 scraped articles.

Projects & Competitions

  • Teaching Assistant, Stanford MLab (Oct 2025 – Present)
    • Advise and participate with student teams on SemEval-2026 (e.g., Task 8: multi-turn RAG evaluation; Task 3: DimABSA; Task 2: temporal emotion modeling), emphasizing rigorous evaluation and clean computational semantics pipelines.
  • NeuralFin (Winner, Murex Best Development Project Award, $3,000, June 2023)
    • NeuralFin: A full-stack research platform (Django, Python) for predictive signals. It features data ingestion, feature engineering, cross-validation, and backtest reporting, designed for rapid iterate→evaluate→ship cycles.
  • Refinitiv Portfolio Management Competition (University Representative, Oct 2023)
